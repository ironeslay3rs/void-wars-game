# Void Wars: Oblivion — Canon Gaps

Known differences between game lore data and canonical novel text.
Items marked **CANON GAP** need author confirmation or future book references.
Items marked **GAME-SPECIFIC** are valid adaptation-layer content with no novel override.

## Doctrine Gaps

- **Fourth Flame (Pure/Ember Vault):** Not explicitly named in Books 1-7. Depth 4 milestone uses placeholder text. Awaiting author confirmation.
- **Sixth Flame (Pure/Ember Vault):** Not explicitly named in Books 1-7. Depth 6 milestone uses placeholder text. Awaiting author confirmation.
- **Mecha doctrine structure:** Only one line confirmed from novels — "Structure holds when members fail" (Book 3). Remaining 6 Mecha doctrine milestones are game-specific creative work.
- **Bio doctrine structure:** Only "The Youngest Day" confirmed as a named doctrine (Book 2). Remaining 6 Bio doctrine milestones are game-specific creative work.

## Character Gaps

- **"The Cook" (Puppy Vol.1):** Real name never revealed in the series. Mama Sol is the game's adaptation of this archetype.
- **Game-specific NPCs (no novel source):** Glass, Kessler-9, Sable, Old Ivory, Root, Ashveil, Nails, Tomo Wrench, The Warden, Hazel. All are game-specific characters designed for district presence.
- **Discount Lars:** CANONICAL from Puppy Vol.1 Episode 1 — confirmed as Bonehowl Syndicate seller of cut Lycan strains.

## Currency Gaps

- **Lesser currency forms:** Veinshards, Heart-Iron, Veil Ash, Meldshards are from the March 17 game design session, not found in novels.
- **Market Sigils (premium currency):** Game-specific concept for USD monetization. Not in novels.
- **Meldheart (fusion apex material):** From session design, not in novels.
- **Core four confirmed:** Sinful Coin (Book 4), Ichor (Books 4-6), Soul Crystals (Books 4-6), Ironheart (Book 2). These are locked canon.

## Terminology Gaps

- **"Puppy" as generic term:** Confirmed for Elias Korr specifically in the Puppy spinoff series. The main novels (Books 1-7) do not establish "Puppy" as general Blackcity slang — it may be Elias-specific. Game uses it as a universal newcomer term.
- **Nation-sin-school mapping:** The Master Guide provides a table mapping 7 nations to 7 sins to 3 schools. Individual nation backstory depth varies across the novels. The game's specific seat titles (Bonehowl of Fenrir, Flesh Thrones of Olympus, etc.) are game-specific.

## Location Gaps

- **District internal structure:** Sin lane names are canonical (Arena of Blood, Feast Hall, Mirror House, Velvet Den, Golden Bazaar, Ivory Tower, Silent Garden). Sub-locations within each lane are game-specific.
- **"Lower Sector 9" and "Cairo Arcology":** Canonical from Puppy Vol.1 as specific Blackcity/Mecha territory details. Not yet referenced in the game.
- **Non-sin districts:** Void Market, Mercenary Guild, Mecha Foundry, Crafting District, Teleport Gate, Faction HQs are game-specific district additions. The novels focus on the 7 sin lanes as the primary structure.

## System Gaps

- **The Chronicle:** A canonical progression system from Book 4 — "game-like structures introduced within reality." Not yet implemented. Our rank/mastery/mythic ladder is a functional equivalent but not canon-named.
- **Mana as a game mechanic:** Canonical from Books 2-6 as a core universal force. Not represented as an explicit resource or mechanic in the game — rune capacity fills a similar role.
