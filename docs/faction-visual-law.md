## Visual Law by Macro School

### Verdant Coil (Bio)
Must communicate:
- Adaptive life.
- Organic aggression.
- Growth through mutation, flesh, bone, root, venom, bloom, or predation.

Avoid:
- Reading as clean mechanical futurism.
- Looking like generic nature fantasy detached from war and survival.
- Blurring into Pure rune mysticism unless explicitly justified by canon.

### Chrome Synod (Mecha)
Must communicate:
- Engineered order.
- Machine authority.
- Precision, hierarchy, structure, command, and industrial power.

Avoid:
- Soft natural silhouettes as primary identity.
- Mystical abstraction that hides the machine lineage.
- Excess ornament that weakens the sense of control.

### Ember Vault (Pure)
Must communicate:
- Rune-centric power.
- Sacred or cultivated force.
- Heat, ash, sigils, vaulting discipline, and concentrated transcendence.

Avoid:
- Reading as standard fantasy priesthood with no forging or rune logic.
- Looking primarily biological or industrial.
- Effects spam that obscures clarity.

## Sin-Led Presentation Cues
These are directional cues, not extra lore.

- Wrath: violent momentum, feral pressure.
- Gluttony: consumption, intake, accumulation.
- Envy: mirrored status, imitation, comparison.
- Lust: allure, temptation, influence.
- Greed: possession, value concentration, acquisition.
- Pride: elevation, authority, dominance.
- Sloth: stillness, delay, inevitability.

## UI and Content Rules
- Faction screens should show meaningful separation between Bio, Mecha, and Pure at a glance.
- Nation labels, sin labels, and seat titles must stay paired correctly.
- Icons, panels, and art briefs should reinforce wartime wear, consequence, and cultivation pressure.
- Presentation should support a serious war-scarred expedition RPG tone, not a playful collector tone.

## Flexible Implementation Space
- Specific color palettes.
- Motion language and animation timing.
- Typography and frame treatments.
- Individual icon sets and environmental prop choices.
- The depth of faction-specific screen treatment at different production stages.

## Review Checklist
Use this before shipping faction-facing assets or UI.

- Is the faction clearly Bio, Mecha, or Pure?
- Are nation, sin, and seat labels canon-correct?
- Does the presentation feel war-scarred rather than cosmetic-only?
- Does it avoid generic gacha flair and reward-first spectacle?
- If a visual cue is new, is it implementation detail rather than invented canon?
