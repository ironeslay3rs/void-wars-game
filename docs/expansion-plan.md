# Void Wars: Oblivion — World Integration & Expansion Plan

## Goal
Use the current world bible to turn the prototype into a scalable content roadmap. This plan maps the existing game shell to the setting's canon pillars:

- Humanity is trapped inside the Void, a prison forged from a failed divine war.
- Three schools drive progression: **Bio / Verdant Coil**, **Mecha / Chrome Synod**, and **Pure / Ember Vault**.
- The seven Sins rule fractured nations and later collapse into larger empire blocs.
- Iron is the central player fantasy: a survivor-rune-smith whose path eventually reveals the forbidden fusion of **body, mind, and soul**.
- Book 2 is the major game expansion pivot where **mana-language spreads**, **guilds and clans reform**, **schools rise publicly**, and the **Chronicle of Ash** enters the world state.

---

## Canon Pillars to Use In-Game

### 1. World Foundations
- Humanity is the shadow of a higher-dimensional species exiled into the Void.
- The Void suppresses divinity and caps power progression.
- Evolution is the surviving divine spark.
- The true path is fusion: **body (Bio), mind (Mecha), soul (Pure)**.

### 2. Faction Spine
The current in-game faction fantasy should align to the three major schools:

- **Verdant Coil (Bio)** — mutation, predation, adaptation, hunt hierarchy.
- **Chrome Synod (Mecha)** — perfection, aristocratic machine order, discipline.
- **Ember Vault (Pure)** — fire, memory, rune-craft, soul-forging, human-centered transcendence.

### 3. Nation / Sin Layer
Each major content region can be tied to one Sin ruler and national identity:

| Nation | Pantheon | Sin | School | Signature Region |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Norway | Norse | Wrath | Bio | The Bonehowl of Fenrir |
| Peru | Inca | Gluttony | Pure | The Mouth of Inti |
| Greece | Greek | Envy | Bio | The Flesh Thrones of Olympus |
| Lebanon | Canaanite | Lust | Bio | The Crimson Altars of Astarte |
| India | Hindu | Greed | Pure | The Thousand Hands of Vishrava |
| Egypt | Egyptian | Pride | Mecha | The Divine Pharos of Ra |
| China | Chinese | Sloth | Mecha | The Clockwork Mandate of Heaven |

### 4. Character Spine
Priority cast to support progression, story, and faction UX:

- **Iron** — player-facing protagonist fantasy; rune-smith, survivor, eventual fusion vessel.
- **Richard** — crafting mentor and tutorial anchor for rune systems.
- **Erick** — black-market / guild escalation contact.
- **Pride** — elite Mecha ruler and relational antagonist.
- **Sloth** — inevitability philosopher; high-level strategic foil.
- **Iron's sisters** — emotional stake, lineage-plunder questline, expansion hooks.
- **Wrath / Envy / Lust / Greed / Gluttony** — future boss rulers and doctrine carriers.

## Expansion 1 — Book 2: Awakening / Seeds of Escalation
This should be the first major content expansion because it matches the strongest source material and the current prototype's feature surface.

### Expansion fantasy
The world wakes up. Mana-language spreads. Guilds reform. Clans rise. The schools stop being hidden subcultures and become visible powers. The Chronicle of Ash appears as the first major Pure-path mystery.

### Feature pillars
#### 1. Mana-Language System
Turn magic into a readable/collectible language progression.

Gameplay uses:
- Unlock rune syntax fragments through missions and factions.
- Translate glyph chains to access dungeons, gates, contracts, and crafting recipes.
- Use syntax combinations as a mid-core progression layer between loot and mastery.

#### 2. Guild Reform System
Upgrade the current guild screen into a living system.

Gameplay uses:
- Join or found a guild.
- Choose guild doctrine alignment: Bio / Mecha / Ember / neutral black-market.
- Guild perks unlock district access, contracts, storage, and shared research.
- Weekly guild campaigns compete for regional influence.

#### 3. Clan & Lineage Layer
Introduce smaller social factions beneath schools.

Gameplay uses:
- Clan passives and crafting bonuses.
- Lineage perks with corruption risk.
- Lineage-plunder questline centered on Iron's sister.

#### 4. Chronicle of Ash Questline
This becomes the first major narrative arc.

Gameplay uses:
- Serialized missions uncover pages, visions, and ember memories.
- Chronicle pages unlock Pure doctrines and Ash-themed professions.
- End-of-expansion raid or boss event reveals the first true Ember Vault threshold.

### New zones
- **Ash Archive** — replacement/evolution of Spirit Enclave.
- **Guild Hall District** — guild management, recruitment, banners, contracts.
- **Clan Warrens** — social hub for lineage, black-market, and rivalry content.
- **Mana Observatory** — language decoding, research, and codex progression.

### New professions unlocked in this expansion
- **Rune-Smith**
- **Mage**
- **Assassin**
- **Warlock** (flagged as dangerous beast-fusion path)

### Monetizable / live-ops safe surfaces
- Cosmetic guild banners and hall themes.
- Rune glyph skins and chronicle page frames.
- Seasonal clan war tracks.
- Battle pass framed as a Chronicle expedition.

## Expansion 2 — Book 3: Fall of the Old World
Purpose: escalate from school emergence to geopolitical collapse.

### Expansion fantasy
Nations collapse, old borders die, new empire blocs emerge, and the Sins awaken as full world powers.

### Feature pillars
- World map opens into seven Sin nations.
- Nation-control events determine resource modifiers.
- PvE warfront campaigns against Sin-aligned forces.
- Faction reputation evolves into empire allegiance.

### Core content
- **Wrath campaign**: Fenrir's battlefield doctrine, endless-war event design.
- **Pride campaign**: Mecha order, command hierarchy, elite trials.
- **Greed campaign**: prosperity cults, debt systems, resource manipulation.

### New game systems
- Regional resource economy.
- War map with rotating frontline bonuses.
- Dynamic world modifiers tied to ruler influence.

## Expansion 3 — Chronicle of Ash
Purpose: deliver the first fully realized Pure-path campaign.

### Expansion fantasy

### Feature pillars
- Memory-forging missions.
- Rune-set crafting.
- Saint Rune awakening at legendary thresholds.
- Ember doctrines and Seven Flames progression.

2. Ash Marks the Survivor
3. Memory is a Blade
4. The Ember Outlasts the Storm
5. The Ember Smothers in Silk
6. The Phoenix Burns Twice
7. The Last Flame is Yours Alone

### Systems unlocked
- Rune set collections.
- Fire-memory relic crafting.
- Chronicle boss gauntlets.
- Ashbound companion units.

- Mecha frame assembly.

4. Memory in Metal

6. The Tower Without End

## Expansion 7 — Beyond the Void

## Recommended Release Order

### Near-term production order
1. **Core Lore Alignment**
2. **Book 2: Awakening / Seeds of Escalation**
3. **Chronicle of Ash**
4. **Fall of the Old World**
5. **Four Thrones / Velvet Trial**
6. **Divine Machine / Synod Ascension**
7. **The Convergence**
8. **Beyond the Void**

### Why this order works
- Book 2 content fits the current prototype best.
- Guilds, professions, factions, and codex systems are already scaffolded.
- Chronicle of Ash gives a strong mid-game narrative spine.
- World-war content should wait until region, economy, and social systems are stable.
- Fusion/endgame systems only work once all three schools feel real and mechanically distinct.

## Success Metrics
A successful expansion roadmap should achieve the following:

- Players understand the difference between Bio, Mecha, and Ember at a glance.
- Book 2 introduces social belonging: guilds, clans, schools, and shared magical language.
- The Chronicle of Ash becomes the first memorable long-form narrative driver.
- Iron's identity is visible in UI, systems, and progression instead of only implied.
- Later expansions stack naturally toward the fusion reveal rather than feeling like disconnected feature drops.

## One-Sentence Direction
**Build the live game around Book 2 first: make the world awaken through mana-language, guild reform, and the Chronicle of Ash, then scale outward into the Sin nations, school empires, and final fusion war.**
