# Black Market Law

## Purpose
This document defines the Black Market as a gameplay-safe lore anchor for hubs, economy, social rules, and neutral-space content.

## Locked Canon
- The Black Market is a neutral survivor citadel between the three empires.
- Deals are sacred; betrayal costs more than death.
- Erick is tied to the Black Market.

## Development Law

### What the Black Market Must Be
- A neutral ground that can host trade, repair, forging, information exchange, and uneasy coexistence.
- A place where survival logic is stronger than faction dogma.
- A stable return point in the core loop: prepare, resupply, recover, and re-enter the war.

### What the Black Market Must Not Become
- A casual shopping mall detached from war pressure.
- A faction-owned capital.
- A reward spam layer full of redundant tabs, currencies, or gacha-style clutter.
- A place where betrayal is trivial, consequence-free, or common in baseline interactions.

## Gameplay Implications
- Hub features should reinforce preparation, scarcity, negotiation, and consequence.
- Trade systems should feel binding and deliberate rather than disposable.
- Vendors, crafters, and brokers should read as survivors operating under strict citadel norms.
- Erick-facing content should connect naturally to hub access, trade networks, or citadel intelligence.

## Flexible Implementation Space
- Exact district layout, traversal flow, and unlock order.
- Which lanes become interactable first.
- Specific NPC rosters, shop inventories, and service menus.
- The exact enforcement mechanisms behind "betrayal costs more than death," as long as the consequence remains severe.

## Implementation Checklist
Use this when adding Black Market content.

- Does this feature preserve Black Market neutrality?
- Does it reinforce survival, trade law, or wartime pressure?
- Does it avoid excess currencies, tabs, or reward-first UX?
- If betrayal appears, is it treated as a major breach rather than throwaway drama?
- If Erick is referenced, does the reference fit his Black Market tie without inventing unsupported lore?
